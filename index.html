Why do you need a project aggregator?

Students participate in tons of competitions, hackathons, and workshops—but all that effort gets buried in scattered folders, certificates, and Google Drives. Hackfolio brings everything under one digital roof.
Recruiters and mentors don’t just want grades—they want projects, initiatives, and impact. Hackfolio helps students present their real achievements in a clean, credible portfolio format.
With one public link, students can share their tech journey with event organizers, interviewers, or even on LinkedIn—without sending 10 PDFs.
Traditional resumes miss the story behind hackathons. Hackfolio shows your challenges, ideas, roles, and growth—something a GPA can't capture.
When students see others’ work, they get inspired to team up, apply for new challenges, or build better next time. Hackfolio becomes a source of motivation.

How Hackfolio helps?

  1. Acts as a Central Portfolio

    Hackfolio collects all your hackathon entries, competition wins, open-source contributions, and project highlights—automatically or manually—and organizes them neatly in one place. Centralize all your hackathon achievements, projects, and certificates

  2. Streamlines Your Story

    Instead of scrambling through certificates, folders, or emails, Hackfolio presents your growth journey—what you built, learned, and accomplished—with a click. Track your hackathon journey with visual stats

  3. Boosts Visibility & Credibility

    Want to show a recruiter or a hackathon organizer your real work? Hackfolio gives you a shareable, professional portfolio link that speaks louder than a resume. Share your portfolio with a single link – no PDFs needed.

  4. Saves Time, Adds Impact

    No more building portfolios from scratch every time. Hackfolio standardizes your achievements, giving you more time to build and create, not organize.

  5. Makes You Discoverable

    When students share their Hackfolios, it creates a network of like-minded creators—others can discover your work, reach out for collaborations, or even invite you to join new challenges. Stand out to recruiters, mentors, and peers
